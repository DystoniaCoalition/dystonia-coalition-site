What we study

Our Projects

Every study the Coalition has run or is running, gathered under one roof — major studies, pilot projects, and career awards, completed and ongoing.

Major studies

Studies that received dedicated, priority funding

These received significant dedicated funding as high priorities for our investigators, patient advocacy groups, and NIH sponsors.

Ongoing

Natural History Project

Long-running study tracking how dystonia progresses over time across participants.

Dystonia Biobank

A shared collection of biological samples supporting genetic and biomarker research.

Patient-Reported Outcomes App

A tool for capturing how dystonia affects daily life, directly from patients.

Soft Signs of Dystonia

Research into subtle early indicators that may aid earlier diagnosis.

Completed

Diagnostic Guidelines for Cervical Dystonia

Established criteria to support consistent diagnosis.

Severity Rating Tools for Cervical Dystonia

Standardized measures for tracking severity over time.

Diagnostic Guidelines for Blepharospasm

Established criteria for this focal dystonia subtype.

Diagnostic Guidelines for Laryngeal Dystonia

Criteria supporting consistent diagnosis of voice-affecting dystonia.

Digital Video-Based Diagnostic & Severity Tools

Rating methods built around standardized video assessment.

Pilot projects

Seed-funded studies

Smaller studies the Coalition supported with seed grants, data, or materials — mostly a historical record at this point. Recent examples include mood screening for cervical dystonia, AI-assisted screening for laryngeal dystonia, sex differences in dystonia, and digital voice analysis.

Career awards

Supporting the next generation of investigators

These awards supported promising young researchers proposing novel ideas for future dystonia research, funded from 2009 through 2022. Examples include work on direct quantification of adductor spasmodic dysphonia, immune mechanisms in cervical dystonia, and non-invasive neuromodulation in task-specific dystonia.
